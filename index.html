Joel Galenson
I am a senior engineer at Qualcomm Research Silicon Valley.

My CV is available here.
Contact me at jgalenso at qti dot qualcomm dot com.

Education

I completed my Ph.D. from UC Berkeley in 2014.
I received a B.S. (with honors and distinction) from Stanford University in 2008.

Research interests

Systems security, return-oriented programming, compilers, program synthesis, static and dynamic program analysis, testing, language design

Current research projects

  • I am researching behavioral mobile security solutions that protect against malware and exploits.
  • I am developing compilation techniques for programming special purpose accelerator architectures.

Past research projects

  • CodeHint: a dynamic program synthesis tool integrated into the IDE.
    CodeHint received second place at LIVE 2013.
  • PBD: A programming by demonstration system that applies some of CodeHint's ideas to help synthesize data tructures or end-user tasks.
  • Research.js: A project to try to get researchers to compile their tools to JavaScript to make demos more easily available. A short writeup is available here.
  • The Compiler Forest: While I interned at Microsoft Research Silicon Valley I worked on a framework to enable modular compiler construction.
  • Yada: A deterministic parallel programming language that aimed to make certain types of parallel programming easy with simple type annotations.
  • Angelic programming: A programming methodology that allows users to refine their specifications into programs.
  • PiVC: A verifying compiler that accompanies the The Calculus of Computation.
